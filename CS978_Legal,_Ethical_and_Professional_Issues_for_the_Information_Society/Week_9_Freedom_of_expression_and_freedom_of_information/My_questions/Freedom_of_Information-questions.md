# Freedom of Information Questions

## Introduction

&nbsp;
<details>
<summary>
1. What does the Freedom of Information (FOI) legislation focus on?
</summary>

FOI legislation focuses on transparency and openness in government and public sector operations, ensuring citizens have the legal right to access information to enhance accountability.
</details>

&nbsp;

<details>
<summary>
2. What is Article 10 of the Human Rights Act, and how does it relate to FOI?
</summary>

Article 10 of the Human Rights Act states that everyone has the right to freedom of expression, including the right to hold opinions and to receive and impart information without interference by public authorities or national frontiers. FOI relates to the latter part, ensuring legal rights to access information from public bodies.
</details>

&nbsp;

<details>
<summary>
3. How does the UN General Assembly define Freedom of Information in Resolution 59?
</summary>

The UN General Assembly declares that "Freedom of Information is a fundamental human right and is the touchstone of all the freedoms to which the United Nations is consecrated."
</details>

&nbsp;
<details>
<summary>
4. When and where was the first FOI legislation introduced?
</summary>

The first FOI legislation was introduced in the USA in 1966.
</details>

&nbsp;

<details>
<summary>
5. What are the key FOI acts in the UK, and what do they cover?
</summary>

- **Freedom of Information Act 2000**: Relates to all UK-level government departments, including Scottish-based departments of UK government bodies.
- **Freedom of Information (Scotland) Act 2002**: Applies specifically to devolved public services in Scotland.
</details>

## Freedom of Information (Scotland) Act 2002

&nbsp;
<details>
<summary>
6. What types of recorded information does the Freedom of Information (Scotland) Act cover?
</summary>

The Act covers all types of recorded information, including hard copies, electronic records (e.g., emails, videos, audio, CCTV), held by Scottish public authorities such as local councils, health authorities, and universities.
</details>

&nbsp;

<details>
<summary>
7. Can FOI requests be submitted globally, or are they limited to UK/EU citizens?
</summary>

FOI requests can be submitted globally and are not limited to UK/EU citizens.
</details>

### Information Request Process

&nbsp;
<details>
<summary>
8. What are the requirements for submitting an FOI request under the Scottish Act?
</summary>

Requests must be submitted in a recorded format (e.g., email, fax, written letter, or recorded phone message) and must include the requester’s name and address (email is acceptable).
</details>

&nbsp;

<details>
<summary>
9. How quickly must public authorities respond to FOI requests?
</summary>

Public authorities must respond to FOI requests within **20 working days**.
</details>

&nbsp;

<details>
<summary>
10. What formats can requesters specify for receiving the information?
</summary>

Requesters can specify formats such as:
- Written summary.
- Electronic format.
- Document inspection on the authority's premises.
</details>

### Example Requests

&nbsp;
<details>
<summary>
11. Give examples of the types of information that can be requested under FOI.
</summary>

Examples include:
- Organizational policies and internal decision-making procedures.
- Financial matters (e.g., departmental pay scales).
- Commercial matters (e.g., buying and selling land).
- Health policies (e.g., NHS service comparisons for postcode lottery issues).
</details>

---

### Costs and Fees

&nbsp;
<details>
<summary>
12. What are the cost caps for providing information under FOI?
</summary>

- The first **£100** of costs is borne by the organization.
- Up to **10%** of costs between £100 and £600 can be charged.
- The maximum fee chargeable is **£50**.
</details>

&nbsp;

<details>
<summary>
13. Is there any obligation for organizations to fulfill requests that exceed £600 in cost?
</summary>

No, organizations are not obligated to fulfill requests if the cost exceeds £600.
</details>

## Responsibilities of Public Authorities

&nbsp;
<details>
<summary>
14. What is the purpose of publication schemes under Section 23 of the Act?
</summary>

Publication schemes outline:
1. Information routinely published by the authority.
2. Methods for accessing the information (e.g., website, printed copies).
3. Whether any associated costs apply.
</details>

## Exemptions

&nbsp;
<details>
<summary>
15. What are absolute exemptions, and can you give examples?
</summary>

Absolute exemptions do not require public interest consideration. Examples include:
- Information accessible elsewhere.
- Confidentially provided information.
- Personal data protected under data protection law.
</details>

&nbsp;

<details>
<summary>
16. What are non-absolute exemptions, and how do they differ from absolute ones?
</summary>

Non-absolute exemptions require public interest consideration. Examples include:
- Health, safety, and environmental issues.
- Commercial interests.
- National security.
</details>

---

## Data Protection vs. FOI

&nbsp;
<details>
<summary>
17. How does FOI handle personal data requests under data protection laws?
</summary>

- An individual’s request for their own data is an **absolute exemption** under FOI and falls under data protection laws.
- FOI requests for identifiable third-party data must adhere to data protection principles and weigh public interest.
</details>

---

## Role of the Scottish Information Commissioner

&nbsp;
<details>
<summary>
18. What powers does the Scottish Information Commissioner (SIC) have in handling FOI disputes?
</summary>

The SIC can:
1. Investigate appeals and issue compliance orders.
2. Serve information notices to public authorities.
3. Mediate between requesters and authorities.
4. Enforce disclosure of information in the public interest.
5. Allow appeals to the Court of Session on points of law.
</details>

FOI legislation ensures transparency, accountability, and openness in public organizations, helping to build trust and empower citizens to scrutinize governmental decisions.
